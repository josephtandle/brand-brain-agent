# Framework: Competitive Alternative
> Source: April Dunford, Obviously Awesome (2019)
> Use for: positioning statement, differentiation, sales conversations

---

## The Core Question

"What would your customer have done if they had never found you?"

This is not "who are your competitors?" That question leads to feature comparisons. This question leads to the real alternative — which is almost never another provider.

---

## The Real Alternatives (Usually)

- Kept doing it themselves
- Hired a VA or assistant
- Bought a course and done nothing with it
- Used a generic tool (Zapier, ChatGPT, etc.) without a system
- Stayed stuck and hoped it resolved itself
- Hired an agency and lost control of their voice

The company that beats you is not your competitor. It's inertia.

---

## How to Apply

**Step 1:** Ask honestly — what would this specific person have done if they had never found me? Do not default to "hired a competitor" unless that is truly the most likely answer.

**Step 2:** Describe why that alternative feels attractive at first. (It must have real appeal or they wouldn't consider it.)

**Step 3:** Name what that alternative cannot provide that you can. This is your actual differentiation — not vs competitors but vs the real alternative.

**Step 4:** Find the sharpest version of that difference. That is your positioning anchor.

---

## The Positioning Statement Formula (April Dunford)

"For [target customer] who [has this problem/situation], [product] is a [category] that [provides this value]. Unlike [real competitive alternative], [product] [does this differently/better]."

---

## Diagnostic Questions

- What is the #1 thing your best client would have done if they had never found you?
- Why does that alternative feel attractive at first?
- What does that alternative give them that you also give them? (table stakes)
- What does that alternative fail to give them that you provide? (differentiation)
- Which of those differences is felt most acutely by the client?
