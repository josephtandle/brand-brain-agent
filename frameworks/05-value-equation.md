# Framework: The Value Equation
> Source: Alex Hormozi, $100M Offers (2021)
> Use for: offer design, pricing, guarantee framing, sales copy

---

## The Formula

**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort and Sacrifice)**

To increase perceived value, you can:
- Increase the dream outcome (make the result bigger or more vivid)
- Increase perceived likelihood (proof, guarantees, testimonials, credentials)
- Decrease time delay (how fast they see results)
- Decrease effort and sacrifice (how easy it is to show up and get value)

---

## The Four Levers

**1. Dream Outcome**
What is the most vivid version of the result? Not "saves time" — "you can take a week off and the business still runs." Not "grows revenue" — "you run a $500k month without a team."

**2. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement**
Why would they believe it will work for THEM? Specificity beats superlatives. A real client story with a real number beats any claim. Guarantees (refund structures) move this lever dramatically.

**3. Time Delay**
When do they first feel a win? "Leave each session with something built" collapses time delay to zero — the first win is in session 1.

**4. Effort and Sacrifice**
What do they have to give up or do? "Show up for 2 hours on Monday" is low effort. "Figure it all out yourself between sessions" is high effort. Live building collapses this.
