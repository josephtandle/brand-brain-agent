# Framework: Lovemarks
> Source: Kevin Roberts, Saatchi & Saatchi CEO — "Lovemarks: The Future Beyond Brands" (2004)
> Academic validation: Journal of Advertising Research, 2006
> Use for: diagnosing brand health, scoring content emotional depth, setting brand ambition

---

## The Love/Respect Matrix

Every brand sits somewhere on this 2×2:

| | Low Respect | High Respect |
|---|-------------|--------------|
| **High Love** | Fad | **Lovemark** |
| **Low Love** | Commodity | Brand |

- **Commodity**: Interchangeable. Bought on price. Price promotions are the only lever.
- **Brand**: Trusted but emotionally inert. Customers choose it but don't love it. Vulnerable to a better-value alternative.
- **Fad**: Loved temporarily but not trusted. Crashes when the novelty fades.
- **Lovemark**: "Loyalty beyond reason." Customers defend it unprompted, pay premium, forgive mistakes, recruit others.

**Diagnostic question for any project**: Where does this brand currently sit on the matrix? What is it missing — love or respect?
