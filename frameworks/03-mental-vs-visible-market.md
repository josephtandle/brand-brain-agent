# Framework: Mental Market vs Visible Market
> Source: Adapted from positioning theory; referenced in Age of AI Workshop
> Use for: homepage copy, pricing strategy, sales conversation difficulty

---

## The Two Markets

**Visible Market**
Features, price, specs, deliverables. Buyers compare. Someone always charges less.
- "6 weekly sessions, resource vault, community chat community"
- "100+ hours saved per month"
- "$450-$999/month"

**Mental Market**
Beliefs, identity, meaning, transformation. Buyers desire. Devoted clients live here.
- "I run a business that runs itself, and it still sounds like me."
- "I'm someone who builds, not just someone who pays for things."
- "I stopped being the operator and became the architect."

---

## The Core Insight

Nobody pays premium prices for features. They pay for:
1. Certainty the outcome will happen
2. Proximity to the person who has the answer
3. The identity of someone who invests at this level

When your positioning is in the Visible Market, your sales conversations are always hard. When it's in the Mental Market, clients close themselves.

**The Ferrari principle:** Ferrari deliberately limits production. They could triple revenue. They choose not to because scarcity is part of the positioning. You do not need 200 clients. You need 20 right ones. Premium pricing is a Mental Market decision.

---

## How to Apply

**Step 1:** Read your current homepage copy or sales page. Underline every sentence. Mark each one V (Visible) or M (Mental).

**Step 2:** Count. If 80%+ is Visible, your positioning is in the wrong market for premium pricing.

**Step 3:** For each Visible Market sentence, ask: "What does having this make the client feel or believe about themselves?" That answer is the Mental Market version.

**Step 4:** Rewrite the hero section with the Mental Market statement first. Features can live below the fold.

---

## The So What Test (bridge to Mental Market)

Take any feature or outcome. Ask "So what?" until you hit an identity statement.

Example:
- "100+ hours saved per month" — so what?
- "More time for high-value work" — so what?
- "I can focus on the people I actually care to serve" — so what?
- "I am someone whose business serves my life, not the other way around." — STOP. That is Mental Market.

---

## Diagnostic Questions

- Are your sales conversations hard or easy? (Hard = Visible Market positioning)
- What do your best clients say when they refer you to someone? (Usually Mental Market language)
- What identity does your client take on when they join? Can you name it?
