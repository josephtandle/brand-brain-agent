# Framework: Anti-Positioning
> Source: Brand Brain Workshop Part 1
> Use for: ICP clarity, sales qualification, positioning sharpness

---

## The Core Idea

"This offer is not for [type of person] who wants [outcome you do not deliver]."

Anti-positioning is not exclusionary. It is clarifying. It signals that you know your client well enough to know who you cannot help. And paradoxically, it makes the right person trust you more.

---

## Why It Works

1. **It signals confidence.** A brand that knows who it's not for has clearly thought about who it is for.
2. **It pre-qualifies.** The wrong person self-selects out before wasting your time.
3. **It makes the right person feel chosen.** "This isn't for everyone" makes the person who it IS for feel like they're in the right room.
4. **It sharpens the positioning.** You cannot name who you don't serve without clarity on who you do.

---

## How to Write It

Complete the sentence: "This offer is not for [type of person] who wants [outcome you do not deliver]."

Then check:
- Is it honest? (Not "not for lazy people" — that's a jab, not a clarification)
- Does it name a real type of person who would otherwise be attracted? (If no one like this would ever come, it's not doing any work)
- Does it make the right person feel more clearly that they are the right person?

---

## Examples

"This is not for people who want someone else to build it for them and never want to understand how it works." (example brand)

"This is not for founders who are still figuring out what they're selling." (example brand)

"This is not for people who want a course to binge and a certificate to hang on the wall." (Any program that prioritizes implementation)

"This is not for people who believe the answer is to keep adding apps." (example brand — names the Common Enemy directly)
