# Framework: Brand Brain Workshop
> Source: Generic positioning, copywriting, and brand operations workflow
> Use for: running a full brand brain build from scratch for any project

---

## Series Overview

This workshop turns scattered brand knowledge into a usable operating system.

It has three passes:

1. **Positioning:** who you speak to, what they want, and why they choose you.
2. **Language:** how to express the positioning so the right buyer feels understood.
3. **System:** how to capture the finished decisions in a brand brain that can guide people and AI tools.

Each pass depends on the one before it. Do not build the language layer until the positioning is specific enough to survive comparison against real alternatives.

---

## Pass 1: Positioning

Run these exercises in order:

1. **Baseline Description Test:** paste current messaging into an AI tool and ask what it believes the brand does, who it serves, and why someone would buy.
2. **Visible vs Mental Market:** separate concrete deliverables from the felt outcome and identity shift the buyer actually wants.
3. **Through-Line Diagnosis:** find the weakest link across positioning, language, leads, and follow-up.
4. **Awareness Level:** identify whether the audience is problem-aware, solution-aware, or product-aware.
5. **Best Client Pattern:** describe the best real client or customer in specific behavioral terms.
6. **Competitive Alternative:** name what the buyer would do if this brand did not exist.
7. **Common Enemy:** identify the belief, behavior, or category habit the brand stands against.
8. **Positioning Statement:** write the specific person, situation, outcome, method, and alternative.
9. **Anti-Positioning:** define who the brand is not for and what expectations it will not serve.

**Output:** an approved positioning statement that passes the specificity test.

---

## Pass 2: Language

Use the approved positioning statement to build usable messaging:

- translate features into felt outcomes with the So What Chain
- turn visible-market descriptions into mental-market language
- write hooks for the audience's current awareness level
- capture the phrases customers use in testimonials, calls, reviews, and support messages
- remove vague superlatives and replace them with concrete proof

**Output:** a message bank with headline options, hook angles, objection responses, proof points, and customer language.

---

## Pass 3: System

Move the decisions into the project brand brain:

- approved positioning statement
- audience pattern and awareness level
- common enemy and anti-positioning
- voice rules and banned phrases
- proof points and testimonial language
- offer description and competitive alternatives
- open questions that still need research

**Output:** a maintained `BRAND-BRAIN.md` that can guide content, sales assets, website copy, and AI-assisted drafting.

---

## Operating Rule

The brand brain is not a one-time strategy document. Every meaningful new testimonial, sales insight, offer change, or positioning decision should either update the file or create an explicit open question.
