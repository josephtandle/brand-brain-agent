# Framework: Jobs to Be Done
> Source: Clayton Christensen, The Innovator's Dilemma + Tony Ulwick
> Use for: ICP deepening, offer naming, content angles, sales conversations

---

## The Core Idea

People don't buy products. They "hire" them to do a job.

The job is the underlying progress the person is trying to make in their life. When you understand the job, you understand why they would "hire" you over any alternative.

---

## The Three Job Layers

**Functional job:** The practical task. "I need to stop spending 40 hours a week on admin."

**Social job:** How they want to be seen. "I want to be the kind of founder who has their systems sorted."

**Emotional job:** How they want to feel. "I want to feel like I'm in control of my business, not the other way around."

Most marketing addresses only the functional job. The social and emotional jobs drive the actual decision.

---

## The Hiring and Firing Questions

**When did they "hire" you?**
What happened just before they decided to move forward? What was the trigger — a specific moment of frustration, an incident, something that pushed them from "I should do this someday" to "I need to do this now"?

**What were they "firing"?**
What were they doing before that wasn't working? (The VA, the course, the manual process.) What specifically disappointed them about it?

**What anxieties did they have before hiring you?**
"What if it doesn't work for me?" "What if I'm too creative/non-techie for this?" "What if I don't have time to implement?"

**What social/emotional progress were they seeking?**
Not just "more time" — "the kind of person who has automated their business."

---

## How to Apply

Interview your best clients. Ask:
1. Walk me through the day or week before you signed up. What happened?
2. What had you already tried? What disappointed you about it?
3. What was the final thing that made you decide to move forward?
4. How did you feel the first week? The first month?
5. How would you describe what you got to someone who hasn't heard of this?

The language from these interviews is your best copy.
