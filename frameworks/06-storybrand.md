# Framework: StoryBrand
> Source: Donald Miller, Building a StoryBrand (2017)
> Use for: homepage structure, email sequences, about page, video scripts

---

## The Seven Elements

Every brand story has the same structure as every movie that works:

1. **A Character** — the customer, not the brand. They have a goal.
2. **Has a Problem** — external (practical), internal (emotional), philosophical (injustice).
3. **And Meets a Guide** — the brand. The guide has authority AND empathy. NOT the hero.
4. **Who Gives Them a Plan** — clear, simple steps. Confusion kills conversion.
5. **And Calls Them to Action** — direct CTA. People don't act without being asked.
6. **That Helps Them Avoid Failure** — name what's at stake if they don't act.
7. **And Ends in Success** — describe the transformation vividly.

---

## The Most Common Mistake

**Brands position themselves as the hero. The customer is the hero. The brand is the guide.**

Yoda, not Luke. Gandalf, not Frodo. The guide has been where the hero is going and knows the way. The guide's job is to equip, not to impress.

This means: stop leading with "We are the #1 automation example program." Start leading with "You built something real. Now let's make sure it runs without you."

---

## The Three Problems

Every customer has three layers of problem:

- **External:** The practical thing that's broken. "I'm spending 40 hours a week on admin."
- **Internal:** The emotional weight. "I feel like I'm failing because I can't figure this out."
- **Philosophical:** The injustice. "A business this good shouldn't require this much of me."

Most brands address only the external problem. The internal and philosophical problems are what create emotional resonance and loyalty.

---

## The Plan

The plan must be simple. Three to five steps maximum. More than that feels overwhelming and kills conversion.

Good plan: "1. Book a call. 2. We build your system together. 3. It runs without you."
Bad plan: "Complete our 14-module onboarding, fill in the intake form, attend orientation, set up your tech stack, complete the pre-work..."

---

## How to Apply to a Homepage

| Section | StoryBrand element |
|---------|-------------------|
| Hero headline | Character + their goal |
| Sub-headline | The problem (internal or philosophical) |
| "Here's how it works" | The plan (3 steps) |
| Testimonials | Success stories |
| "Without this..." | Failure stakes |
| CTA button | Direct call to action |
