# Framework: The Promise Ladder
> Source: Positioning and copywriting theory
> Use for: homepage hierarchy, CTA sequencing, content progression, offer stacking

---

## The Core Idea

A Promise Ladder is a sequence of claims that escalates from immediately believable to aspirational — each rung building on the credibility established by the one before it.

Leading with your biggest claim first (before trust is established) feels like hype and triggers skepticism. Leading with your smallest claim and building up earns trust before making the big ask.

---

## The Four Rungs

**Rung 1 — The Believable Small Win**
Something the reader can immediately verify or accept as plausible. Low stakes. High credibility.
Example: "You'll leave each session with something built."

**Rung 2 — The Tangible Medium Outcome**
A result that requires some trust but is supported by the small win. Specific numbers help.
Example: "Most members save 100+ hours a month within 60 days."

**Rung 3 — The Significant Transformation**
A real life or business change. Requires established credibility to land.
Example: "Members stop being the operator of their business without hiring a team."

**Rung 4 — The Identity Shift**
The aspirational felt outcome. Only lands if the first three rungs are solid.
Example: "I run a business that runs itself, and it still sounds like me."

---

## How to Apply

Map your current homepage or sales copy to the rungs. Then ask:
- Are you starting at Rung 1 or jumping to Rung 4?
- Is each rung supported by proof before you make the next claim?
- Is your Rung 1 specific enough to be immediately believable?

---

## Common Mistakes

- **Leading with Rung 4:** "Transform your business and your life!" — This is noise before trust is earned.
- **Skipping Rung 2:** Going from a small win straight to identity shift loses the through-line.
- **Generic Rung 1:** "You'll learn valuable skills" — vague, unbelievable, forgotten immediately.
