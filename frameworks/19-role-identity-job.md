# Framework: Role Identity Job (JTBD Brand Layer)
> Source: Clayton Christensen, Tony Ulwick, Bob Moesta — Jobs to Be Done theory
> Brand application: SIVO Insights, Strategyn
> Use for: ICP deepening at brand level, positioning, content strategy, community building

---

## The Three Job Levels

| Level | Type | Question it answers |
|-------|------|-------------------|
| 1 | Functional Job | What practical task am I hiring this to do? |
| 2 | Emotional Job | How do I want to feel when I use this? |
| 3 | Role Identity Job | Who do I become by associating with this brand? |

Most products compete at Level 1. Most premium brands compete at Level 2. **Lovemark and cult brands compete at Level 3.**

AI can deliver Level 1 results. Anyone can deliver Level 2 feelings. Only a brand can deliver Level 3 identity.

---

## The Role Identity Job Defined

The role identity job captures the aspirational role a person is trying to live into through their behavior and purchasing decisions.

This is not "I want to feel confident" (emotional job). This is "I want to be someone who builds instead of buys" or "I want to be someone whose business doesn't need me to be there every day."

The role identity job is:
- A future self, not a current state
- A social signal, not a private feeling
- An identity, not an outcome
- What they would tell someone else about themselves if asked

**Key insight**: When a brand delivers the role identity job, it creates a fundamentally different relationship than when it delivers the functional or emotional job. The customer becomes the brand's ambassador because they are now advertising their own identity — the brand is just the symbol of it.

## How to Find the Role Identity Job

**Interview method (Bob Moesta's "Switch Interview"):**

1. "Walk me through the day you decided to move forward. What happened?"
2. "Had you thought about this before? What stopped you from acting earlier?"
3. "What had you already tried? What disappointed you about it?"
4. "When you imagined things being different, what did that look like?"
5. "How would you describe yourself to someone who asks what kind of business you run?"
6. "When you tell a friend about what you're doing now, how do you describe it?"

Questions 5 and 6 surface the role identity job directly. The language the customer uses to describe themselves to others is the role identity job.

---

## The Role Identity Job in Brand Positioning

**Functional positioning**: "Saves 100+ hours a month."
**Emotional positioning**: "Feel in control of your business."
**Role identity positioning**: "Be someone who runs a business that runs itself."

Role identity positioning is the only layer that creates loyalty beyond reason. When the brand becomes part of how someone describes themselves, switching costs become identity costs — not just functional or financial ones.

---

## How to Apply to Brand Content

For each piece of content, ask:
1. Which job layer is this content serving?
2. If it's only serving Level 1 (functional), rewrite to activate Level 2 or 3.
3. Does the CTA reinforce the role identity? ("Join us" reinforces belonging. "Start your automation" is functional. "Become someone whose business runs while they sleep" is role identity.)

**The role identity CTA formula**: "Become [the person who has achieved the transformation]" — not "get [the result]."
