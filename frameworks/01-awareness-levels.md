# Framework: Five Levels of Awareness
> Source: Eugene Schwartz, Breakthrough Advertising (1966)
> Use for: content strategy, hook writing, ad targeting, nurture sequences

---

## The Five Levels

| Level | Name | Mental state | What they think |
|-------|------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | Unaware | No problem registered | "This is just how things work." |
| 2 | Problem Aware | Feels friction, no name for it | "Something isn't working but I don't know what." |
| 3 | Solution Aware | Knows solutions exist | "I need better systems / clarity / positioning." |
| 4 | Product Aware | Knows YOUR offer | "I've seen this. Not sure yet." |
| 5 | Most Aware | Ready to buy | "I'm ready. Who do I hire?" |

---

## The Core Insight

Most businesses pitch at Level 5. Most of their audience is at Level 2 or 3. That is why content does not convert: the message does not match the moment.

The most valuable clients — loyal, long-term — are built at Levels 2 and 3. When you help someone understand their problem and then their options, they trust you deeply by the time they are ready to buy.

---

## How to Apply

**Step 1:** Identify what level your audience is at when they first encounter your content. This varies by channel:
- Cold ads/explore: typically Level 1-3
- Organic followers: typically Level 3-4
- Referrals: typically Level 4

**Step 2:** Match your content's job to that level:

| Level | Content's job | Hook style |
|-------|--------------|-----------|
| 1-2 | Name the problem they feel but haven't named | "You are probably doing X and wondering why Y." |
| 3 | Show the path from problem to solution | "Here is what actually fixes X." |
| 4 | Confirm fit, collapse hesitation | "Here's what it looks like on the inside." |
| 5 | Make it easy to take the next step | Clear CTA, low friction |

**Step 3:** Never write a single piece of content for all levels. Know which level you're writing for before you write.

---

## Diagnostic Questions

- What does your ideal client type into Google or say to a friend the week before they find you?
- What level is that?
- What level is most of your current content speaking to?
- Is there a gap?
